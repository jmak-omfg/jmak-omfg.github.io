Active research projects

Mesoscale eddy parameterization

In its present form, GEMOETRIC is an eddy energy constrained mesoscale eddy parameterisation, i.e. the standard eddy induced transport coefficient scales with a distribution of the parameterised eddy, which itself evolves according to some equation (see for example the GEOMETRIC NEMO notes). We have been instrumental in getting the GEOMETRIC code into some ocean general circulation models (e.g. MITgcm + NEMO, repository to come), and demonstrating how GEOMETRIC has led to some improvements in the modelled ocean climatology sensitivity.

There are a few areas of investigation this group is leading in relation to GEOMETRIC (see also some work led by David Marshall):

  • parameter inference from an optimisation point of view
    -- collaborators: Tomos David (ENS Lyon), Alexandros Avdis (Imperial) Han Seul Lee and Yongsu Na (HKUST)
  • state estimates and dynamical parameter inference
    -- collaborators: Yongsu Na (HKUST)
  • impact on OMIP projections
    -- collaborators: George Nurser (NOC Southampton), David Marshall (Oxford)
  • mesoscale activities and parameterisation over slopes
    -- collaborators: Yongqi Liu and Yan Wang (HKUST)
  • time-scales associated with the GEOMETRIC parameterisation
    -- collaborators (sort of): Georgy Manucharyan
  • parameterisations with internally consistent energy cycles

Ocean modelling

We create toy, idealised and realistic ocean models to answer a variety of problems in dynamics as well as those in biogeochemical and ecological context, which will be made publicly available in due course. We are relatively agnostic with the choice of models, and usually the scientific questions dictate the models to be used; the main ones we use so far are Firedrake, NEMO, MITgcm, GETM.

  • inference problems for South China Sea circulation (Firedrake and MITgcm w/ AD tools)
    -- collaborators: Han Seul Lee and Yongsu Na (HKUST), Matt Mazloff (Scripps)
  • vertical resolution, internal waves, and ecological consequences (NEMO)
    -- collaborators: Bobo Ruan and Alex Wyatt (HKUST)
  • South China Sea circulation and global circulation interactions (NEMO w/ AGRIF)
    -- collaborators: Bobo Ruan (HKUST)
  • tracer dispersion and connectivity in the Lagrangian framework (NEMO w/ Ariane and/or OceanParcels)
    -- collaborators: Bobo Ruan, Floriane Sudre, Alex Wyatt (HKUST), Chris Wilson (NOC Liverpool)
